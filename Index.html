MORE INFO

PARENTS

For all the parents out there, our goal is only to enrich your child’s experience at your school of choice. It’s either go 100% art, 100% science, or most times, don’t go for either.

A large issue with many public schools is the limited options when it comes to extracurriculars, and most schools don’t have an equal distribution between arts and science.

Here at STEAM Academics, our idea is to promote science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics equally. This will provide your children with the choice between a number of fields, and hopefully make them more open to learning even further information in the future.

STUDENTS

As for the students, it is imperative that you try to spread the message of STEAM Academics to your peers once you read all about what STEAM is and how it can benefit your future.

Having choices is the most important thing when you are in school, so why not have the choice of expanding your horizons rather than being cornered into one area of extracurriculars?

Continue looking through the site and learn more about STEAM and the specifics involved, then consider bringing STEAM up to your parents and teachers.

TEACHERS

The information on our site is arguably most important for the teachers to know, because you will be the ones with the most say in how STEAM could be integrated into your schools.

By integrating STEAM programs into your schools, you as the teachers will have more choice in what subjects you would like to teach, because there are vastly more topics contained within STEAM than there is in any public school curriculum.

Please browse through the rest of the site for more information, and contact any of the team members with any further questions and how to get started.

PROGRAMS

ENGAGEMENT

A key component when talking about STEAM with your schools is to increase engagement on the topic itself.

STEAM programs incorporate this by having hands on workshops that the students can participate in and grow their knowledge and skills with.

IMPACT

Impact is very vital when considering STEAM programs in your schools.

Impact can be seen and felt through different interactive activities like workshops. Implementing these programs can lead to a higher level of impact within your schools.

SUSTAINING

Arguably the most important aspect of advocacy is sustaining STEAM programs.

This is done by having an open dialogue about STEAM at your schools, making sure that new generations of students and teachers don’t forget about STEAM as an option.

STEAM vs STEM

STEAM vs STEM

As far as STEAM goes, the general public may not be familiar with the term, and are probably more familiar with STEM.

The core difference as you may have noticed, is the A for arts in STEAM.

This seemingly minor change makes a world of difference between the two types of programs by opening the doors to any student, not just the particularly math or science inclined students.

CHANGING THE NORM

There is a massive mountain to be scaled when it comes to changing the public perception and knowledge of STEAM programs.

The first step in changing the norm would be to share and promote sites like STEAM Academics and others.
